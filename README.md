# Awesome CapCut Alternatives [![Awesome](https://awesome.re/badge.svg)](https://awesome.re)

> Free and paid alternatives to CapCut, organized by what you actually need. Updated for the 2026 CapCut Pro restructure.

In early 2026 CapCut moved several previously-free features (auto-beat-sync, advanced AI effects, 4K export, full effects library) behind the Pro tier at $19.99/mo. This list tracks the real alternatives — not affiliate-bait — by use case.

## Best free browser alternative

**[Cutflux](https://cutflux.app)** — Browser-based, no signup, no watermark. Built around automatic beat detection. Best if your goal is short-form edits that cut to music. Doesn't try to be a full NLE — it's focused. Free with no paid tier.

**[Clipchamp](https://clipchamp.com)** — Microsoft-owned. Full NLE in the browser. Free tier exports up to 1080p. Better than Cutflux if you need a general-purpose editor, weaker if you specifically need beat-sync.

## Best free desktop alternative

**[DaVinci Resolve](https://www.blackmagicdesign.com/products/davinciresolve)** — The single best free editor that exists. Full color grading, audio mixing, motion graphics. Free version has no watermark. Learning curve is real.

**[Shotcut](https://shotcut.org)** — Open-source, lighter than Resolve, no learning cliff.

**[Kdenlive](https://kdenlive.org)** — Open-source, Linux-native but cross-platform.

## Best for beat-sync editing

CapCut's headline feature was beat-aligned cuts. Replacements:

- **[Cutflux](https://cutflux.app)** — Auto-beat-sync in the browser, free, no login.
- **DaVinci Resolve** — Manual beat markers via Fairlight. More work, more control.
- **Premiere Pro** — `M` to set beat markers, snap clips. Industry standard but paid.

## Best for auto-captions

CapCut's auto-captions were one reason people stayed. Replacements:

- **[Submagic](https://submagic.co)** — Paid ($16+/mo). Specialist tool. Best caption styles.
- **[Captions](https://www.captions.ai)** — Paid. Mobile-first.
- **[Veed.io](https://veed.io)** — Browser, paid.
- **DaVinci Resolve** — Free auto-captions built in (Speech-to-Text feature).
- **[Whisper](https://github.com/openai/whisper)** + ffmpeg — Free, technical, gives you SRT files to overlay.

## Best for repurposing long-form

For turning a 30-minute video into short-form clips:

- **[OpusClip](https://www.opus.pro)** — Paid. AI-driven clip selection.
- **[Klap](https://klap.app)** — Paid. Similar.
- **[Vizard](https://vizard.ai)** — Paid. Long-form to short.

## Best for AI-generated video

CapCut's AI Auto-Edit feature suggests cuts from raw footage. Replacements:

- **[Veed.io Pro](https://veed.io)** — Browser, AI text-to-video.
- **[Descript](https://www.descript.com)** — Script-to-video.
- **[Runway](https://runwayml.com)** — Generative video.

## Decision tree

- **You want to keep editing in a browser, free, no signup**: Cutflux for beat-sync, Clipchamp for general.
- **You want full pro power, free, willing to learn**: DaVinci Resolve.
- **You're a long-form creator clipping podcasts/streams**: OpusClip or Klap (both paid).
- **You're making short-form viral edits with music**: Cutflux for the cut, then style in another tool if needed.
- **You need captions above all else**: Submagic if you can pay, DaVinci Resolve if you can't.
- **You're an AMV or AMV-style editor**: DaVinci Resolve + Cutflux for first-pass beat alignment.

The 2026 CapCut Pro change pushed many short-form creators to look for alternatives. Most "alternatives" lists are affiliate spam. This is a flat, honest reference — what each tool actually does, organized by what you actually need. PRs welcome to keep it current.
